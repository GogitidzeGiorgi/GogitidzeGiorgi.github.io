GRE® General Test Content

An overview of the test sections, timing, and tasks.

Test overview

The GRE General Test has three section types: Analytical Writing, Verbal Reasoning, and Quantitative Reasoning. The total test takes just under 2 hours to complete, but you should plan for 2.5 hours to allow 30 minutes for check-in. The test includes one Analytical Writing section, two Verbal Reasoning sections, and two Quantitative Reasoning sections.

Test sections overview

Use the table below to view the breakdown of each GRE General Test section type.

Section Estimated Timing Questions/Tasks Description
Analytical Writing 30 minutes 1 "Analyze an Issue" task Write an essay in response to a prompt that presents an issue of general interest and asks for your perspective.
Verbal Reasoning 41 minutes (across 2 sections) 27 questions (across 2 sections) Measures your ability to analyze written material and synthesize information. Includes Reading Comprehension, Text Completion, and Sentence Equivalence questions.
Quantitative Reasoning 47 minutes (across 2 sections) 27 questions (across 2 sections) Measures your problem-solving ability using basic concepts of arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data analysis. An on-screen calculator is provided.
