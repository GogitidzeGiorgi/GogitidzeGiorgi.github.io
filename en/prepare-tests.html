Prepare for Tests

Register and prepare for TOEFL, IELTS, GRE/GMAT.

Stage II: Academic and Test Preparation

1. Language Learning and Preparation

  • Choosing IELTS/TOEFL/DET: Check which exam the university accepts.
  • Study methods: Consider attending courses, online lessons, a private tutor, or self-study.
  • Test dates: IELTS/TOEFL are held multiple times a year; it's important to take the exam in time for the score to be ready before application deadlines.
  • Practice tests: It is essential to take simulation tests to practice time management.

2. Standardized Tests (SAT, ACT, GRE, GMAT, etc.)

  • Checking requirements: Not all universities require the SAT/ACT. For example, some universities in the USA have become test-optional.
  • Registering for exams: SAT/ACT registration should be done at least 1–2 months in advance.
  • Preparation strategy:
    • SAT: Reading, Writing & Language, Math (Calculator / No Calculator), Optional Essay.
    • ACT: English, Math, Reading, Science, Optional Writing.
    • GRE/GMAT (for Master's programs): Required for business, economics, and some technical programs.
  • Results timeline: Scores are released 2–3 weeks after taking the exam, so it's necessary to consider the timing.

3. Collecting Academic Documents

  • School documents:
    • Transcript of grades – Must be notarized and in English.
    • Certificate or Diploma – Must also be translated and notarized.
  • University documents (if applying from Bachelor's to Master's):
    • Grade sheet, diploma, and any relevant certificates.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Inform your teachers or professors in advance so they have enough time to prepare.
  • Additional documents:
    • Certificates from courses, competitions, or olympiads.
    • Letters from employers if work experience is important for your application.
