GMAT™ Focus Edition Content

An overview of the test sections, timing, and tasks.

Test overview

The GMAT Focus Edition has three sections: Quantitative Reasoning, Verbal Reasoning, and Data Insights. The total test takes 2 hours and 15 minutes to complete, but you should plan for about 2 hours and 45 minutes to allow for check-in and an optional 10-minute break. You can choose the order in which you complete the three sections.

Test sections overview

Use the table below to view the breakdown of each GMAT Focus Edition test section.

Section Estimated Timing Questions/Tasks Description
Quantitative Reasoning 45 minutes 21 questions Measures your ability to use logic and analytical skills to solve problems with arithmetic, algebra, and geometry.
Verbal Reasoning 45 minutes 23 questions Assesses your reading comprehension and critical reasoning skills to evaluate arguments and formulate a plan of action.
Data Insights 45 minutes 20 questions Measures your ability to analyze and interpret data from multiple sources to make informed decisions.
