Stage III — Application Preparation (5-9 Months)

1. Personal Essay / Personal Statement

Main Idea: The essay should show who you are as a person and why you are a good fit for the specific university.

  • Choosing a Theme: A single strong thread that unifies your story (e.g., "Leadership in the Community," "Investigative Thinking," "Creative Vision").
  • Creating the Structure:
    • Introduction: An attention-grabbing story or fact from your life.
    • Academic Motivation: Why you chose this field.
    • Personal Experience: Extracurricular activities, volunteering, projects.
    • Future Goals: How this program will help you with your plans.
    • Conclusion: A brief summary and a strong ending.
  • Writing Process:
    • First Draft: Focus only on the ideas.
    • Editing: Removing unnecessary information, adding specifics.
    • Feedback: From a teacher, mentor, or friend.
    • Final Version: Clear, 500-650 words (Common App standard).
  • Tips:
    • Show, Don't Tell: Don't just say "I am a leader," show it with an example.
    • Avoid clichés ("I have been fascinated by X since childhood…").
    • Use concrete results ("I attracted +30% more participants to the club").

2. Resume / CV

Main Idea: A one-page "picture" of your academic and personal journey.

  • Education: School/University, GPA (if high), important subjects.
  • Experience:
    • Work or Internship: 3-4 bullet points for each.
    • Use the "Action → Result" style: "Organized X project, which led to Y result."
  • Extracurricular Activities: Clubs, sports, music, volunteering.
  • Achievements/Awards: Competitions, Olympiads, certificates.
  • Skills: Foreign languages, computer programs, other special skills.
  • Format:
    • Maximum 1-2 pages.
    • Clear headings and bullet points.
    • Save in PDF format for universities.

3. Letters of Recommendation

Main Idea: To show the university your academic and personal side from an "outside perspective."

  • Choosing a Recommender:
    • A teacher who has known you for ≥1 year.
    • An employer or project leader, if extracurricular experience is important.
  • Preliminary Communication:
    • Ask at least 2-3 months in advance.
    • Explain where you are applying and what the deadlines are.
  • "Brag Sheet" (Brief information about you):
    • Your achievements and activities.
    • Specific examples that can be highlighted in the letter.
  • Format:
    • 1 page.
    • Official tone.
    • Specific examples ("[Name] was one of the most active students in the class, his/her presentations always stood out...").
  • Quantity: Most programs require 2-3 letters of recommendation.

4. Portfolio (if the program requires it)

Main Idea: For creative or practical fields (art, architecture, design, music).

  • Check the requirements: Not all universities require one.
  • Selecting Works:
    • The best 10-15 samples.
    • Show both the finished work and parts of the process.
  • Structuring: Give each work a title, date, and a brief description.
  • Format:
    • PDF (≤50MB), online gallery, or video presentation.
    • Professional look, with a minimalist design.
  • Presentation Rules: Start with your strongest work and also end with a strong one.
