IELTS Test Content

An overview of the test sections, timing, and tasks.

Test overview

The IELTS test has four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. The total test takes about 2 hours and 45 minutes to complete. The Listening, Reading, and Writing sections are all completed in one sitting. The Speaking test may be taken on the same day or up to a week before or after the other sections.

Test sections overview

Use the table below to view the breakdown of each IELTS test section.

Section Estimated Timing Questions/Tasks Description
Listening 30 minutes (+10 mins transfer time) 40 questions Listen to four recordings (conversations and monologues) and answer questions.
Reading 60 minutes 40 questions Read three long passages and answer a variety of question types. Texts differ for Academic and General Training tests.
Writing 60 minutes 2 tasks Describe visual information or write a letter (Task 1), and write an essay (Task 2). Task 1 differs for Academic and General Training tests.
Speaking 11–14 minutes 3 parts Have a face-to-face conversation with an examiner, including short questions, speaking at length on a topic, and a discussion.
