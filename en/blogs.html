Giorgi Dzadzamia
Physics • Vanderbilt University • Full Funding

About Giorgi

Giorgi was accepted to Vanderbilt University (USA) in Physics with full funding! Giorgi's choice was influenced by his love for physics, physics classes, physics club, and the school environment, while his decision to continue studying in the United States was motivated by unlimited opportunities, quality education, and the diverse international university environment.

At Vanderbilt University, which is among the top 15 universities in the United States according to US News, only 4 Georgian students study. All four Georgian students continue their studies with full funding and all four of them are Komaroveli alumni.

Achievements and Activities

  • Physics circle teacher at Komarovi
  • European Physics Olympiad Silver Medalist (2020)
  • Winner of Silver (2020) and Bronze (2019) medals at Zhautikov International Physics Olympiad
  • Under Giorgi's team leadership, Georgia's national team won: 4 Bronze medals at the International Olympiad, 2 Silver and 3 Bronze medals at the European Physics Olympiad
  • Interests include fantasy literature, storytelling, and creative board games
Irakli Mataradze
Molecular Biology • Wingate University • $200,000 Scholarship

About Irakli

Irakli was accepted to Wingate University with full $200,000 funding and continues his studies in Molecular Biology and Biochemistry! Additionally, Irakli received 100% funding from the leading North Carolina university in Chapel Hill.

Irakli achieved a perfect score of 800/800 on the SAT mathematics section and scored 8.0/9.0 on the IELTS exam, which speaks to his exceptional academic abilities.

Achievements and Activities

  • Teacher in the "Fun Mathematics" project
  • Voluntarily teaches mathematics to Georgian and foreign students
  • Perfect SAT Mathematics score: 800/800
  • IELTS score: 8.0/9.0
  • Passionate about studying biological engineering and genetic modifications
  • Studies multiple languages: Russian, Armenian, English, Japanese, and German
Lasha-Giorgi Shamugia
Aerospace Engineering • Princeton University • $250,000 Scholarship

About Lasha-Giorgi

Lasha-Giorgi Shamugia was accepted to Princeton University with $250,000 funding! Princeton University is one of the most competitive and prestigious universities in the Ivy League, America, and the world.

Lasha plans to continue his studies in aerospace and mechanical engineering. As he says, aviation and the idea of interplanetary travel have always fascinated him, and his knowledge and love of physics and mathematics made choosing his future profession even easier.

Achievements and Activities

  • Successful student of Komarovi's physics circle
  • National Educational Olympiad finalist in Physics and Mathematics in 2019, 2020
  • Initiator and organizer of the innovative volunteer project "Math Meetings" - prepared 14 sixth-graders for 5 months
  • EducationUSA Georgia Competitive College Club student and delegate of the first international student congress (2020)
  • 2021 semifinalist of the international writers' competition "Between the Lines" (IWP BTL)
  • Former soloist of the folklore group "Shavnabada" and participant in many local and international events

Nika Tskitishvili
Astrophysics • NYU Abu Dhabi • $330,000 Scholarship

About Nika

Nika Tskitishvili was accepted to New York University's Abu Dhabi campus (NYUAD) with full $330,000 funding in the Astrophysics faculty!

New York University (NYU) is distinguished by high quality and international education. NYU has three main campuses in New York, Abu Dhabi, and Shanghai. The Abu Dhabi campus is home to students from more than 120 different nationalities.

Achievements and Activities

  • Administrator of Georgia's Astronomy Club (GAC). Organizes astronomy evenings and online lectures for more than 32,000 astronomy enthusiasts
  • Organizer of Georgia's first astronomy festival "Astrofest-2020", which was funded by Shota Rustaveli National Science Foundation of Georgia
  • Member of Georgia's national team at the International Olympiad on Astronomy and Astrophysics (IOAA) in 2018-2019
