Research Universities

Identify target universities and programs abroad.

Step 1: Defining Goals

  • Analyzing your interests – Which subjects do you love the most? What are you strong at?
  • Career direction – Where do you see yourself in 5–10 years (e.g., doctor, programmer, architect, researcher)?
  • Study format – Do you want an academic (theoretical) education or a practical-oriented program?
  • Country selection – Culture, lifestyle, climate, university quality, and career opportunities.

Step 2: University Research

  • Program comparison – Each university's course description, subjects taught, and practical opportunities.
  • Rankings and reputation – QS, Times Higher Education, US News rankings.
  • Tuition fees – Annual payment, additional expenses (labs, books, faculty fees).
  • Scholarships and financial aid – University's internal scholarships, government programs, funding from international organizations.
  • Student life – Campus size, clubs, and employment services.

Step 3: Checking Qualification Requirements

  • Academic scores (GPA/average score) – What is the minimum score required for a specific program?
  • Subject requirements – Some universities require specific subjects to be passed (e.g., for medicine – biology and chemistry).
  • Standardized tests – SAT, ACT, IB, A-levels, or internal country exams.
  • Language proficiency level – IELTS, TOEFL, Duolingo English Test.
  • Additional requirements – Some programs require a portfolio, a letter of motivation, or a preliminary interview.

Step 4: Planning Deadlines

  • Application formats:
    • Early Decision (ED) – Typically in November, if the student is already decided on a specific university.
    • Early Action (EA) – Also early (November-December), but it is not mandatory to enroll in a specific university.
    • Regular Decision (RD) – January–February.
    • Rolling Admission – The university accepts applications throughout the year until the spots are filled.
  • Marking on a calendar – The student should create their own "application calendar" where they mark all deadlines.
  • Additional deadlines – Scholarship applications, housing reservations, visa application time.
